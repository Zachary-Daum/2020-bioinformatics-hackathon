    abstract "Photooxidation and hydrolysis are the two primary aging factors
 of intraocular lenses. Opacifications, dislocations, glistening and yellowing
 of the implanted acrylic lenses, which are due to chain scissions and
 depolymerization, are the consequences of aging from the clinical
 perspective. The purpose of this study was to examine the consequence of the
 aging of intraocular lenses on chemical and surface properties. Acrylic
 lenses made of poly acrylic-co-polystyrene polymer were artificially aged by
 photooxidation and hydrolysis from 2 to 20 years. Degradation products were
 observed by Reverse-phase High-Performance Liquid Chromatography RP-HPLC and
 thermogravimetric analysis (TGA). The surface, which was analyzed by atomic
 force microscopy (AFM) and fibronectin adhesion kinetics, was chosen as an
 indicator of intraocular biocompatibility. Low-molecular-weight degradation
 products (LMWP) result from chain scission under both hydrolysis and
 photooxidation. The osmotic effects of water enable degradation products to
 migrate through the polymer. A portion of the degradation products exudate in
 the surrounding center, whereas a portion link with lateral chains of the
 polymer. At the same time, the surface roughness evolves to externalize the
 most hydrophilic chains. As a result, the fibronectin adhesion level decrease
 with time, which indicates the existence of a biocompatible kinetic for
 implanted intraocular lenses.",
